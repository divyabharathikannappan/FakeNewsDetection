Released in 2015, the book delves into the life and legacy of Caitlyn Jenner's father, William H. Jenner, and the profound impact he had on his family.

Beckett, a renowned author and journalist, spent years researching and interviewing members of the Kardashian-Jenner family to gain a deeper understanding of the man who played a significant role in shaping their lives. In "The Thing That Made Us," he explores the values and principles that William Jenner instilled in his children, including Caitlyn, and how they continue to influence the family's success today.

The book also examines the challenges that the Jenner family faced in the 1970s and 1980s, when Caitlyn was still known as Bruce, and how her father's unwavering support helped her navigate the complexities of growing up as a member of the Kardashian family. As Beckett notes, "William Jenner was more than just a father figure – he was a guiding light who helped shape the person his children have become."

He continued, "I wanted to explore the thing behind the Kardashian phenomenon, and for me, that thing was William Jenner's influence on his children."

Since its release, "The Thing That Made Us" has received widespread critical acclaim and has become a must-read for anyone interested in the Kardashian-Jenner family's story. As much as the book offers a fascinating glimpse into the family's past, it also serves as a testament to the enduring power of parental love and support.
